01 — Color

A small palette,
used with discipline.

Red is the signature — it's in the logo and it's what makes Security Molding recognizable from across the room. Black and pure white do the rest of the work. Light gray and sky blue are accents only — used sparingly, like water lines in a mold.

Primary

SM Red #C10000 · R193 G0 B0 Pantone 186 C (approx)
Ink Black #0A0A0A · R10 G10 B10 For text, structure, contrast
Pure White #FFFFFF · R255 G255 B255 Default surface, breathing room

Accents

Light Gray #F2F3F5 Section fills, dividers
Sky Blue #4AA8E0 Water lines, info callouts
Deep Red #8E0000 Hover, emphasis, depth
Whisper Red #F7E3E3 Quiet red backgrounds
Do

Let red lead. Use it for the logo mark, the eyebrow tags, the one thing that matters on the page. White around it.

Don't

Don't paint whole sections in red. Don't mix red with warm oranges or pinks. Don't use red for body copy.

Rules

  • Headings tight. Body relaxed. Never the reverse.
  • One font per heading. No mixed weights in the same line.
  • Max line length: 66 characters. Long lines lose the reader.
  • Uppercase only for mono labels and eyebrows.

03 — Logo

The shield and the rocket.

The mark is a red shield with an upward spike — strength pointing forward. It's drawn at a single stroke weight. Don't re-draw it. Don't color it anything but red or the approved monochromes.

04 — Voice & Tone

We'll take care of it.

Security Molding is three generations of shop floor. The voice sounds like someone who knows the machines by name and would rather fix the problem than explain it. Plainspoken. Warm. Never corporate.

We sound like

  • A phone call that ends with "Yeah, get me the drawing, I'll call you back this afternoon."
  • Short sentences. Active voice. "Here's what we'll do."
  • Technical when it earns its place. Plain otherwise.
  • Comfortable with its own experience — not defensive, not boastful.

We do not sound like

  • A LinkedIn post. No "synergies," no "solutions partner," no "robust."
  • A catalog page. No adjectives stacked up ("world-class, innovative, premier…").
  • An apology. Even when something goes wrong, the move is forward.
  • A tech startup. We're not disrupting anything. We've been here a while.
Write this

"Small molds, small parts, tight tolerances. Been doing it since the 70s. Send the drawing — we'll tell you what we see."

Not this

"Security Molding is a full-service, customer-centric injection molding partner leveraging decades of industry-leading expertise to deliver innovative plastic solutions."
